Binary

These squares show the Unix time, which counts the seconds since 1970.

In 2038, 32-bit clocks like this will overflow, and the two billion towers of time will be solved.

Polygons

These polygons show the time in the format yy:M:w:d:h:mm:ss, where M is the month (empty polygon for January and full polygon for December), w is the week in the month, and d is the day of the week (empty polygon for Monday and full polygon for Sunday).

Blobs

Inspired by blobmaker, I made some blobs which change in time. The leftmost blob has a travelling wave that passes the top once per millennium. The following blobs have waves that pass once per year, day, hour, minute, and second, respectively.


The final blob has all six of the above waves on it.

Solar system

This clock shows orbits of the Earth and Moon, as seen from the perspective of the North Star. The zodiac constellations are fixed in the night sky.

Shaded lines show the Earth's axial tilt at 10° intervals. The June and December solstices are at the top and bottom of the orbit; they have tilts of ±23.5°.
